Terrain Generation:

* early tests: creating a mesh and deforming it
* different methods of terrain seeding and generation
	* will be seeded from a sample of heightmap data
* joining multiple segments cleanly
* giving it adjustable curvature
* texturing it (based on height and steepness)


General Tools:
	* noise - different types
		* vertical noise, like static
		* horizontal deformation of geology
		* masking (so it only appears in certain spots)
	* flat terrain
		* rolling hills and domes
		* volcanic features - old flat lava flow, small mounds, pāhoehoe
		* shallow rounded valleys
		* ripple waveforms, including ones crossing at angles
		* wrinkles (concave or convex)
	* valleys
		* smooth-ish rolling valleys, like elongated hills
		* canyons with flat bottoms, like a pair of cliffs
		* deep chasms
		* cracks
		* grabens
	* craters - common, and smaller ones can be made up
		* rim height (vs surroundings)
		* bowl shape - shallow or deep, center flatness
		* rim sharpness - shape of crater rim
		* ejecta - radial lines
		* shock lines - concentric shock circles
		* inner wall slope
		* outer wall slope
		* overprinting - smaller craters overlapping bigger ones
		* degradation - sharp and fresh, bland and eroded, filled in, collapsed
	* cliffs - usually not very dramatic
		* usually at a dropoff to a lower area
		* long winding scarps
	* mountains
		* slope steepness
		* conical or elongated
		* lone mounts or massive chains
	* surface clutter
		* rocks
	* textures:
		* color and lightness balance
		* detailed rock textures that blend






Parameteriztion:
	* most features have properties that decide their size, shape, and appearance
	* most planets will have a range of values for these properties, dictating that planet's look
	* map features will influence feature frequency and values
