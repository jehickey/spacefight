* it's meant to test LOD at different distances, especially as higher LOD becomes an option



Development Sequence:
	* increasing and decreasing sphere mesh detail
	* swapping between low and high res textures on approach
	* switching to divided spheres with multiple enhanced textures (and balancing memory load)
	* vertex deformation of the spheres to show terrain
	* generation of sections of higher detail terrain (w/ generation)
	* successively higher detail as camera approaches closely

Deformation of the sphere:
	* hysteresis - so it doesn't flicker at the boundary (such as 3 radii)
		* should always be a factor of boundary radius, which itself is a factor of distance (even if hardcoded for now)
	* deterministic base map to guide deformation
		* use one or more 2d maps, terrain or otherwise, to guide randomization for terrain deformation
		* "deterministifc macrostructure"
	* increasing scale height with proximity
		* in between deformation (each time complexity increases)
		* landscapes slowly rise from the sphere instead of popping into place
		* updated at rapid intervals, not every frame (maybe 20-30 frames per second vs full framerate)
